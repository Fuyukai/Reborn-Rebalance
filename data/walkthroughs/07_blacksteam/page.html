Catching Up On Events

Go up to the top of Beryl Ward to the Beryl Cemetery and talk to the spirit. If you have a Mabostiff, she'll give you a {{ sp("GREAVARD") }} (this is a custom event, added by this mod!!). You could smash the rocks and go into the cave... but it's dark! You don't have Flash yet. I know where to find it, though, so make your way back down to the Obsidia Slums.

{{ ss("piggystation2") }}

Well, you can't make it all the way as Cain will jump out at you at the gate to Peridot Ward and drag you into the piggy station. Some cutscenery ensues... and you have to go to another factory in Peridot Ward. Fuck that! We're going back to the slums and you can't stop us!

Rock Smashing About

It's time to collect all of the events that we've missed! There's a lot, so bare with me here.

There's a few areas here in the Slums that we want to go to, actually. First up is the Slums Playground, which is a secret area hidden between the two escalators on the left side of B1F. (You should probably stock up on Repels first). You can unlock the door if you have the Dull Key and then proceed into a jumping puzzle room.

{{ ss("jumpingarrows", "I'm already tired of these puzzles.") }}

Follow the red arrow to find... a Level 45 Pangoro. Activate the switch on the wall and then head down the right set of stairs to get a Sun Stone. Go back and follow the blue arrow to find... another Level 45 Pangoro. Again, activate the switch on the wall. Go down the stairs and follow the green arrow to find... a level 50 Pangoro! After beating him, the Scrafty you beat earlier will come and beat up all the Pangoro gang, and you'll get a {{ sp("SCRAGGY") }}.

Once you're done, you can go back to the other hidden door on the other side of the basement floor and travel down to the area with the two smashable rocks. Break them open to end up in... a lower section of the Underground Railnet! Go right to pick up the Marowakite and a Light Ball, and then smash open the wall directly in front the entrance. (You can't go up yet.) Enter it to find a narrow hallway that ends in another Rock Smashable wall... which opens out into another small corridor. Smash the wall again to find the Scrafinite. Press the switch on the wall and proceed to the right.

{{ ss("darkrailnet", "Dark. Darker. Yet, darker.") }}

This is a dark area... which is very annoying, as we're going down here to find Flash. Go straight to the left until you bump into a wall and you'll find a set of stairs below you. Go down these stairs, along a corridor, and down the stairs again to the lowest floor. Align yourself with the bannister of the stairs, walk straight up til you hit a rock, and then move one tile right. Go up again to find a Rock Smashable wall; break it open to find TMX8 Flash. If you have easy HMs on, when you leave the area again it'll be illuminated fully; otherwise, you'll need to teach Flash to a Pokémon and use it outside of battle.

With the ability to actually fucking see, you'll find a Dusk Stone and another breakable wall that has a House Key within it. You really should pick this up. If you headbutt the middle pole, you can farm infinite Arons. Go back up the stairs to find the Noctowlite now in plain sight, as well as some guy lost in the Railnet; talk to him and he'll skedaddle back upwards. (He has your next sticker, by the way.) Finally, there's a breakable wall up to the top that has an Ability Capsule in it.

You can skedaddle out of the slums now; there's nothing interesting here anymore (and there never will be). Make your way back to the Obsidia Pokécentre and talk to the guy you just rescued (to the left of the counter) to get the Aron sticker. Next stop is the Onyx Ward.

{{ ss("gamblor", "It's Dragon Quest time.") }}

Now that you have the Coin Case (well, you had it a while ago), you can go into the arcade and start gambling. Interacting with a slot machine will open the slot machine minigame; as far as I can tell, you literally cannot lose with these. Just wager the max amount and spam stop and you'll make a thousand coins in ~10 mins. Talking to the various NPCs might give you some extra coins on top of that as well. The Voltorb Flip minigame is safe by design but a bit tedious; the roulette minigame is a money sink.

You actually want to go slightly to the right and talk to the lady loitering outside the locked house. The key you found earlier is actually hers! Give the key to her and she'll reveal herself to be the Move Relearner. For only one Heart Scale she'll reteach any move that Pokémon used to (or could have) known. If you have the SWM Modpack installed, she can also teach egg moves! Her house also has TM103 Pay Day. You can mine Heart Scales from mineable rocks, or later on there will be a way to get them in exchange for Shards.

The move tutors at the school aren't available yet... but if you go up to the second floor and find the Minecraft player, you can convince him to play GregTech irl under the Obsidia Staircase. He'll move out of the way of the computer, and you can insert a Data Chip to find and catch a {{ sp("PORYGON") }}. If you travel up to North Obsidia Ward again, you'll find a summoning circle situated below the staircase; talking to the leader at the top will let you buy a Link Stone for 10 thousand dollarpounds. It won't let you evolve the Porygon, though - you don't have an Up-Grade yet!

Joining A Gang
Whilst this is technically optional right now, we need access to the alleyways that the gang members occupy to get an important Pokémon that we'll need very soon. No matter what, you have to join one of the gangs to progress the story further later on anyway. Good writing, there.

Whilst we're loitering in Obsidia, it's time to join a gang! There's two gangs here in Reborn City; the Aqua Gang and the Magma Gang! It's up to you which one you want to join, as the story is very similar in both. The Aqua Gang are found in the Lapis Ward alleyway, just to the right of the orphanage; the Magma Gang are found in the North Obsidia Ward alleyway, to the right of the nightclub. (You should go talk to the guy outside that, as well.)

{{ ss("baller", "Welcome to a life of crime.") }}

Talk to the two grunts guarding the entrance to either area twice which will spark a double battle in order to join it. In the Aqua alleyway is TM41 Torment, and in the Magma alleyway is TM46 Thief (don't worry, you'll be able to get both soon). Introduce yourself to the gang leader, who tells you that you're all going to meet up at an abandoned shack. I hope you read those road signs!

You're going to need to go into the house directly to the the right of the flower shop in Lapis Ward; a bit away from the wall overlooking Peridot. Talk to the leader for some info on who your target is: an old Tory cunt! Next, go to the house directly below the Pokémon centre (you might have been here before and shooed out) and travel straight through to the back of the room. Your target are the Pokéballs on the table; the left-most one contains an evolution stone (Water Stone for Aqua, Fire Stone for Magma) and the middle one contains a Pokémon (Ponyta for Aqua, Buizel for Magma). Talk to your leader again and skedaddle back to the respective alleyway.

If you talk to your gang leader now, he says that he'll need some time to think about the next actions. Of course, you already beat the third gym, so just talk to him again to get the next quest: the Gang Raid quest. Find the other gang's hideout, beat the hell out of their leader. Now you have free access to both alleyways, which is essential. You can go back and talk to your gang leader to get either a {{ sp("CARVANHA") }} or a {{ sp("HOUNDOUR") }} as a gift, each with a special egg move.

{{ ss("murkrow", "Our secret weapon.") }}
This is not an optional encounter. There's a very important fight coming up soon that you must win, but it is extremely unlikely that you could beat it normally. You're going to need to cheese it.

Before returning to the Aqua Gang alleyway in Lapis Ward, set the time to night and the weather to windy. Save in front of the Murkrow inside the alleyway, catch it, and soft reset with F12 if it doesn't have Prankster. Yes, you need prankster. Yes, you need to do this.

Finally, before returning to Beryl, go back into the house and talk to the old bitch; then exit again. The piggy will come outside and give you a Bike Voucher, which you can use in the bike shop below to get a cool bike. Also, I didn't realise this til I was looking in the editor, but one of the NPCs in the Pokémart will give you TM7 Hail.

{{ ss("icyarrows", "Technically, you've just crossed two regions to get here.") }}

Now, we can return to Beryl Ward; first, go to the Abandoned Power Plant. I'm sure you already tried going down there, but rock smash the rock in front of the cave entrance next to enter Citrine Mountain 2F. The bulk of the mountain is blocked off by a Strength rock (you don't have that, yet), but if you follow the red arrow you will find a {{ sp("TOXICROAK") }}!

The next stop is Beryl Cave (again), above the cemetery. Now armed with Flash, you can actually navigate through it! Within the cave you'll find a set of two Nuzleaf cages (huh?); the left one contains a lever that will open the door to the right one. Within you'll find Corey's {{ sp("NIDORINA") }}! I highly recommend you take the Nidorina.

Exiting out at the very bottom of Beryl Cave, you'll be spat out in a secret area within the Rhidochrine Jungle (you might have seen this on some screenshots earlier). A bunch of Nuzleaves are hanging about and a Shiftry is standing in front of a totem pole. If you interact with the Shiftry, it'll clear out once you beat it in a battle. Now you can interact with the Totem Pole for two possible interactions:

  • If you have an ill-fated doll, you will battle either an {{ sp("ELGYEM") }}, a {{ sp("BALTOY") }}, or a {{ sp("GOLETT") }}.
  • If you don't, and the weather is clear, you will fight a {{ sp("NATU") }} instead.
If it's clear, you don't have a doll, and Natu isn't appearing; for some reason there's a switch that explicitly disables the Natu event. I don't know what enables or disables this switch. If you talk to the rock next to you, it'll disable the switch and let you trigger the event. Use with caution!

Blacksteam Factory

 {{ ss("blacksteam", "I did it, Kim! I teleported!") }}

Now, we can actually continue the story. Make your way to the second factory building at the far left of the Peridot Ward. First, talk to Ace and pick a nature to get a mint. Talk to Amaria for a cutscene, and then proceed inside to fight a double battle (next to her hilariously terribly named and overpowered gym battle team).

Proceed forwards to get another set of cutscenes, whereupon you'll be knocked into a trapdoor and into a room with the most tedious puzzle known to humankind. (If you have the NG++ mod installed, it'll just skip it for you if you've done it before. Bless. But I have to do it again anyway to write out the steps.)

The Pokémon here need to be interacted with in a specific order in order to break open the bars to your right. The steps are basically a loop of "make noise, teleport pokemon, make noise again, make guard fall asleep, use pokemon". So, let's do this in order. The most important Pokémon here are the Loudred (in the far right), the Abra (top centre), and the Jigglypuff (bottom right).

{{ ss("ihatethispuzzle", "Welcome to my unspecial hell.") }}
Puzzle solution
  1. First, to heat up the bars, interact with the Pokémon in this order:
    1. Loudred
    2. Abra
    3. Cyndaquil (bottom left)
    4. Loudred
    5. Jigglypuff
    6. Cyndaquil (centre cage)
  2. Next, to cool down the bars, interact with the Pokemon in this order:
    1. Loudred
    2. Abra
    3. Cyndaquil (centre cage)
    4. Abra
    5. Vanillite (top left)
    6. Loudred
    7. Jigglypuff
    8. Vanillite (centre cage)
  3. Finally, to actually break the bars, interact with the Pokémon in this order:
    1. Loudred
    2. Abra
    3. Vanillite (centre cage)
    4. Abra
    5. Roggenrola (bottom right)
    6. Loudred
    7. Jigglypuff
    8. Roggenrola (centre cage)

Optionally, you can also get a {{ sp("DITTO") }} after this. Talk to Abra, then Roggenrola, then Abra, then Vanillite, then Abra, then Makuhita. Talk to the Makuhita in the former Vanillite cage for Ditto to pop out. Once out of the cage, talk to the Grunt for an optional battle. The path forks into two here; to the left is a set of stairs that leads to a PULSE read-out and TM49 Echoed Voice (you need to slip out to the south).

After that, continue onwards to the right path and up the stairs. Here, the first room at the top contains a Super Potion and the second room contains a computer that will unlock the front gate (meaning you can now exit and go to a Pokécentre, if needed.) To the south is a passage leading to a pair of gates; the computer here unlocks the frontmost one. Go up the stairs to the second floor.

Again, there's a few possible exits here. The lower left room contains a Light Shard, the lower right room contains a large EXP Candy; the upper left room contains a Thunder Stone and the upper right room contains a computer that will unlock the second door downstairs. Once you go in there, you will find another cutscene and then a boss battle with ZEL and PULSE Muk.

{{ trainer_battle_content("ZEL", "ZEL", 2) }}

This thing is insanely bulky on the special side, so focus on physical attacks. It also has Gen 7 Protean, so be aware of its type every time. Once you put the Muk out of its misery, you get the Harbour Key which will give you access to Apophyll Island. There's nothing left for us here in Reborn City for quite a while, so head to the Coral Ward and open the unopenable warehouse to proceed.
