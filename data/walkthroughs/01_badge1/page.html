Badge #1

{{ ss("portraits") }}

Welcome to the Reborn Region! The first thing you need to do is to fill out your name and character portrait. I recommend naming yourself "Leavanny". Most of the portraits have a bad case of Fetal Alcohol Syndrome - something that seems to plague Pokémon games - so I also recommend picking either the second or last portrait.

Passwords

You'll board the train towards Reborn City through the Toumaline Desert, and you will be asked for "special instructions or passwords". Say YES here, because this is where you can enable certain QOL functionality that the game ridiculously doesn't have enabled by default. There's a few passwords you absolutely should enable no matter what; and a few that I prefer to be enabled, but it's up to personal taste.

Must-haves

  • easyhms - enables Gen 7 style HMs; that is, you don't need to have a Pokémon learn a HM to be able to use it outside of battle. Why this isn't the default I really don't know.
  • hardcap - enables a hard level cap. Pokémon won't obey you beyond the level cap anyway, so this just means you can't accidentally overlevel and fuck yourself.
  • weathermod - lets you just set the weather. Waiting multiple real-life days to get the right weather for an encounter or evolution is not engaging gameplay.
  • unrealtime - similar to above, lets you just set the time.
  • antidote - enables Gen 5 style poison damage; that is, none at all outside of battle. Fuck that.
  • freeremotepc - gives you a Gen 8 style box link significantly earlier. The most cheaty of the essential codes, but let's be honest, it's no worse than the standard Gym Strat of running back to the PC after every battle.
  • pinata - Free EXP candy from the trainers in the Grand Hall. This just saves a ton of time compared to grinding them out. Not for true JRPG fans.

Good to have

  • freeexpall - Gives you Gen 7+ style EXP Share right away. Past a certain point you'll just be using EXP Candies anyway, but this is good for the early game.
  • shinycharm - With the Shiny Charm, shiny rates are boosted so that it takes around ten to fifteen minutes of encounters to get a shiny. I think that's a fair tradeoff.
  • fullivs - I'm not grinding for fucking bottle caps.
  • noitems and stopitems - No Hyper Potion cheese in battle. For a more competitive experience.
  • fieldnotes - You'll just look them up online anyway.
  • norolls - Removes damage rolls from the game, so every move does the same damage every time. This gives less "Unfortunate" moments.

You can also enter any type name as a password, which will let you get some good Pokémon much earlier than normal, via early game events. You'll get an explanation of what events these are as you can access them. These passwords must must be entered during the starting sequence, as certain parts of your RNG is baked into the game as soon as you enter the Grand Hall.

I highly recommend you pick one of these codes instead of leeaving it up to chance and getting an Elekid and a Ducklett.

  • monowoke - Porygon (starter), Bronzor (Slums), Staryu (Mystery), Zizagoon in Opal Ward
  • normal - Starly (Mystery)
  • fire - Vulpix/Larvesta/Litwick (Mystery)
  • water - Azurill/Mareanie/Staryu (Mystery), Ducklett (Slums)
  • grass - Shroomish/Cottonee (Mystery)
  • electric - Elekid (Mystery), Pachiritsu in Opal Ward (yikes!)
  • ice - Sneasel/Vulpix-A (Mystery), Cryogonal (Traded)
  • poison - Mareanie/Gastly (Mystery)
  • fighting - Shroomish (Mystery)
  • ground - Trapinch (Mystery)
  • flying - Starly/Togepi (Mystery), Ducklett (Slums)
  • bug - Larvesta (Mystery)
  • psychic - Staryu (Mystery)
  • rock - Rockruff (Mystery), Carbink (Traded)
  • ghost - Gastly/Litwick (Mystery)
  • dragon - Axew/Trapinch (Mystery)
  • steel - Pawniard (Mystery), Bronzor (Slums) (probably the best choice)
  • fairy - Azurill/Togepi/Vulpix-A/Cottonee (Mystery), Mime Jr/Carbink (Traded)

Welcome To Reborn City

♫ Pokémon Reborn - Peridot Ward

{{ ss("peridot_1") }}

After entering all of your passwords, the train you're on will crash into the station shortly after you jump out through the window. You'll wake up... in the Peridot Ward of Reborn City. Sadly, all of the other portraits on the train but you were killed.

You then meet your first leader - Julia - who talks like a baby about explosions. She asks you if you're challenging the Reborn League (yes) and then runs away. You're now free to explore the city!

Well, not really. Most of Lower Peridot Ward is blocked from you to stop you from walking up and getting into battles with no Pokémon. Talk to everyone here for some nice flavour text - you should be talking to absolutely everyone you see, anyway - and proceed to the right to make it to the Opal Ward.

{{ ss("shinycharm") }}

Talk to the purple-haired person at the bottom next to the river to get a single Potion. It doesn't work on humans, but she knows that! There are two staircases to either side, but they're blocked off by two trainers who won't let you past unless you have a Pokémon. If you used the early shiny charm password, there will be an item to the side of the middle staircases that contains it.

You have nowhere else to go but up the middle staircase and into the Grand Hall, where you will meet Victoria, your first rival! She'll take you inside the Grand Hall to the registration desk (hilariously, you walk a little bit faster than she does so this sequence looks pretty glitchy). And now it is time for.....

Choosing Your Starter
In the starter room, you will find a Sunkern Switcher. You must switch teams, to the All-Gen Teams, as those are the ones editeed by the balance mod. Additionally, I don't provide any guarantees that the original teams will still exist, causing crashes or other issues.

Reborn is a hard game. Every major boss battle has a team that hits hard, and usually has a cheaty field effect that directly advantages their type and shuts down the most common counters.

The starter Pokémon are usually some of the best Pokémon available until the encounter tables open up around the third badge. All starters are also available via either the wild or events so you don't have to worry about missing out on any of them.

Whilst you can get away with any starter, some are very obviously better than others.

  • {{ sp("froakie") }}, {{ sp("scorbunny") }}, or {{ sp("sprigatito") }} are great choices overall due to their ability Protean which is one of the best abilities in the game. That said, Sprigatito is especially weak to some of the first gyms; you can get it via event at Spinel Town after the 5th Gym, after which it is one of the best mons available.
  • {{ sp("torchic") }} used to be the best starter hands down, but with the removal of Speed Boost it loses a lot of its previous advantage. You can get it via event near the end of the game.
  • Starter Porygon with the monowoke password, Starter Eeevee with the eevee password, or starter Alolatales with the vulpixpls password also make great choices.

Beyond that, the game relies very heavily on team composition; don't expect to coast along with an overlevelled starter like the main series games! You'll usually end up switching out your starter for a lot of the boss fights anyway.

Grand Beginnings

Once you've picked your starter, you'll be led back downstairs and Victoria will go upstairs to pick her starter (always a Litten). Unfortunately, your second rival - Cain - will barge in, corner you, make inappropriate sexual remarks at you, and then force you into a battle. At least it's an easy battle this time. I think there should be an option to use your Pokémon to target him directly and kill him permanently.

After beating him, Victoria comes back, victim blames you for Cain's sexual comments, and then has another battle with you.

After the battle has finished, you have free movement again and can explore! If you used the shiny charm or remote PC passwords, they will be found as items to the immediate left of the Grand Hall. Don't forget to pick them up before you leave.

At the very bottom of the Grand Hall, next to the exit, there are two NPCs. The one to the right will offer to sell you a rare Pokémon for only 500 dollarpounds; spoilers - its a Magikarp. You won't get this Magikarp for a very long time, either - not until nearly the end of the game. To his credit, in the Reborn region, Magikarp is actually extinct; so he's not lying when he says its rare. It's your choice to go for it or not.

If you do choose to buy it, it'll be stolen immediately. No refunds!

If you've installed the NG+ mod correctly, then there will be a Silver Shard at the bottom left of the Grand Hall. It doesn't do anything except tell you its installed.

Opal Ward

Exit the Grand Hall to the south. Before you can go anywhere you're treated to a quick explanation about the soft level cap and disobedient Pokémon - the specific Pokémon varies depending on your save between either an {{ sp("STUFFUL") }} or an {{ sp("SNUBBULL") }}. Stufful isn't available again until Route 1 (via Bewear), and Snubbull isn't available again until after the 13th Badge.

If you enabled the hard level cap, then Pokémon simply won't level past your level cap. This makes it a whole lot less annoying as you don't have to worry about over-levelling and having to use Common Candies to bring the levels back in line.

On both sides of the Grand Hall is a small wild area. There's nothing here really worth wasting your first set of Pokéballs on, but there is a single Green Shard available if you interact with the furthest left statue. Battle the single trainer, he tells you about Hidden Abilities and how they appear on wild Pokémon in Reborn. This isn't strictly true because hidden abilities have been (mostly) removed in favour of just adding them to the regular ability list, but nevertheless. Go back down the stairs to the south.

On the bridge itself are some battleable NPCs; actually, the bridge slowly gains newer NPCs as the first part of the story progresses. This makes for a fun surprise when you're traversing back to the Grand Hall to get more EXP candies. There's also a set of guys bullying a Pokémon; this is either a {{ sp("ZIGZAGOON") }} or a {{ sp("PACHIRISU") }}. Upper Lower Peridot

Next, above the Pokécentre, there is a Doxy trainer who will battle you. Defeat her, and then talk to her again to get a Rose Incense. You can also interact with the rock below to get a single Antidote. The house to the left directly above has an event {{ sp("SKITTY") }}, although I can't paticularly recommend you bother with that.

To the right is a conspicious alleyway guarded by a single trainer. Defeat him to gain access to the alley; inside, you will find a Super Potion guarded by another trainer. This alley also has some very decent earlygame encounters, like {{ sp("TRUBBISH") }} and {{ sp("STUNKY") }}.

Even further to the right, just before the upper entrance to the Opal Ward, is a flat with a {{ sp("KRICKETOT") }} in it. I highly recommend you get this Pokémon, as Technician Kricketune can carry you really hard throughout the first few gyms, even in the base game. Just remember that its not a good Pokémon for most of the later game, unfortunately, even with the stat boost.

A small area in Opal Ward is accessible to you by following the railway tracks to the east. It has a single trainer in it, and in rainy weather one of the elemental monkeys is available. If you keep going to the right, you will enter the Underground Railnet - or the very entrance. The rest is blocked off by a breakable rock.

There's a bug with easyhms enabled that sometimes lets you use certain HMs early, like Rock Smash and Surf. It doesn't happen all of the time and I don't know how to trigger it, but if you can use Rock Smash on the rock you'll enter a subarea with Level 30 Pokémon and most certainly get wiped.

Finally, to the left of the train tracks up to North Peridot Ward is a building with a flower field next to it. Go inside, beat up the old man, and you'll get access to the garden. The only thing worth catching here is a {{ sp("SWABLU") }}, which has Cloud Nine that can majorly fuck certain story battles up by cancelling their weather effect.

North Peridot Ward

{{ ss("charm") }}

Follow the train tracks up into North Peridot Ward. You will want to move further up in the ward to find a vendor that sells Pokésnax, which you can buy three of to get the rest of the event Pokémon. But first, the way, enter this house to get the TM for Charm. You can also go left into another alley, where you will find a Data Chip guarded by two other trainers.

If you travel all the way north to the gate, on days with clear weather (or if you have weather mod enabled and set the weather to clear), you can use the Rose Incense you found earlier to catch a {{ sp("BUDEW") }}. I once again strongly recommend you get this, as Growth Roselia is really really good at these early stages of the game.

Now, we're almost done with all of the available event Pokémon. If you have the weather mod, set the weather to Rainy or Stormy and interact with the fountain to the right of the railway to catch a {{ sp("SURSKIT") }}. Finally, set the time to night, go down to the left of the house with Charm in it, and enter the building to catch a {{ sp("JOLTIK") }} (provided you have a Pokésnax.)

{{ ss("pokesnax") }}

In clear weather, this guy will appear and will sell Pokésnax for 650 dollarpounds, letting you go back and get all of the event Pokémon that you missed. With that, you've finally caught all of the event Pokémon available to you! For now. (Hehee).

Mosswater Industrial

 {{ ss("incel_fern") }}

Now, it is time to finally advance the plot along again. Go to the east of the Pokémart in North Peridot Ward to find this factory; talk to the green-haired guy. He turns around to tell you to fuck off, and the guy he was interrogating runs away. He introduces himself as Fern (your third Rival), spouts some shit about how he's an Alpha Male, and forces you into a battle. He's a pushover, with his most dangerous Pokémon being his Sneasler; his team is mostly focused around cheesing you with flinches from Bite.

{{ trainer_battle_content("Fern", "Hotshot", 20) }}

After beating him into the ground, another piggie comes along and also tells you both to fuck off. Your next destination (after healing up) is the other factory building in North Peridot Ward that you probably passed on the way up. It's just off the train tracks above the area transistion. Fern meets you there, Julia shows up to blow up the door and make another bad sex joke, Fern's sister (Pokémon Sheldon) shows up to talk about how it's a bad guy hide out (duh). Just hold down text skip for this one, really. You can then enter the building for your first dungeon. It's a double battle dungeon with Fern as your partner!

{{ ss("blacksteam_1") }}

Your only possible route here is to the north, so go through the doorway. As you proceed slightly upwards, you'll be presented with a cutscene with the Meteor Leader - Ace - talking about data deletion or some shit. There's a reference to the Windows 7 copy dialogue, some fourth wall breaking shit, just generally a bad scene overall. Hold down textskip again for this.

The "puzzle" here is to walk around and find screens with numbers on in order to fill in the code to the elevator door to your immediate right. Strictly speaking, you can sit there and bruteforce it if you wanted to; hell, you only even need three (or two if you're patient) digits and can bruteforce the last one in a minute or two. But in the spirit of Grinding, let's battle everyone here. Each one is a double battle alongside Fern, whose team is identical to his previous battle.

This is a good chance to explain Field Effects. Field Effects are special effects that happen to every single battle in a certain map. They buff certain moves (usually always to your opponents advantage), nerf certain other moves (nearly always counterplay to their type), and can change into other fields when using certain moves. You can think of them as a boss battle difficulty modifier. Their effects are explained in more detail inside Field Notes, which you can find throughout the game (or if you used the password, right from the start). As an example, Mosswater Industrial uses the Factory Field - one of the most common fields - that boosts Electric type attacks universally, boosts some steel moves, and can change into Short-Circuit Field when some Ground-type moves are used.
  1. The obvious first step is to your immediate right. Battle the trainers, interact with the green monitor to get the third digit of the code: 7.
  2. Going upwards (we're doing this clockwise), battle the trainers, interact with the right-sided monitors to get some lore (ominous...), and then interact with the left-sided monitor to get the second digit of the code: 1.
  3. Next, go across the bridge to the right. The next digit, the first one, is available in a little cutout at the top: 3.
  4. Finally, go through the small gap to the right of there and go upwards to get the final digit: 9.
{{ ss("mosswater_2") }}

You can then cut back downwards to make it to the elevator door, where you enter the full code: 3179. Go into the elevator for a small set of cutscenes where the power is cut, you're offered a job, the power is restored, and Ace makes a tactical retreat. It's not immediately obvious here but the left, right, and bottom sides of this room are doorways to the catwalks you saw previously. Going in the second downwards exit leads you to a room with a single Small EXP Candy. You're going to need another set of codes here (very creative puzzle design), so...

  1. First, go out the left exit and cross the platform all the way to the left. There will be another set of monitors in the room with the first digit of the code: 4. It should go without saying that all of these rooms have trainer battles in them.
  2. Next, return to the central platform and go to the south room; the monitor in this one will give you the second digit of the code: 8.
  3. Return all the way to the right across three rooms to find another monitor. This gives you the last digit of the code: 2.
  4. Finally, return to the security room and talk to Florina, who will give you the third digit of the code: 6.

The code entrypoint is to the top of the central platform; the final code is then 4862. Enter it, then enter the room to get the explosives. Return back to Julia, who will prime the explosives.... and the door will open by itself. Good thing you didn't waste your time bruteforcing the code, eh? Go through the door and across the catwalk to confront Team Meteor. Again, text skip this one because it really kinda sucks. You then fight two named grunts with their own cut out - almost a boss battle! (But they only have two Pokémon vs your nine.)

Now, you're almost ready to actually challenge the first gym! But before that, let's head back down to the purple-haired pharmacist at the bottom of Peridot Ward, below the Old Rod house. Talk to him for a small cutscene where some guy nicks his opiods and fucks off; you need to retrieve them! He's up in the alleyway next to the train tracks that you should have visited earlier; battle him there and return to the creepy doctor to get 5 Small Exp Candies.

Gym #1

♫ GlitchxCity - Pokémon Mystery Dungeon: Sky Tower Remix

Whilst this Gym is the easiest in the game by some margin, it's certainly not easy. You should make sure all of your Pokémon are at the level cap; use EXP Candies from the trainers in the Grand Hall to quickly level them up.
{{ ss("julia_gym") }}

It's time to collect your first gym badge. Take your time to absorb the absolutely banging music, and then begin by dismantling the gym trainers. I also recommend the tried and tested strategy of "running away to heal between every single battle". You can use the telecom at the beginning to talk to Ame to get some advice about the gym you're about to face, although it often ranges from "useless" to "actively detrimental". The puzzle here is pretty simple; you simply lead the Voltorb in each indent up to the gates and interact with them to blow them up. (Damn, this gym must be expensive to run!)

Finally, enter the uppermost room to meet Julia and battle her. Remember to save at the bottom of the room with LTRIGGER! You can use F12 to quickly reset if your battle strategy isn't working out. She's a complete pushover; I basically rolled over her with my starter first try (Fuecoco). But if you have a different starter, here's a few other strategies you can use team composition wise:

  • Technician {{ sp("KRICKETUNE") }} with Bug Buzz completely rolls over her, except for Oricorio.
  • Growth {{ sp("ROSELIA") }} can take most of her resisted hits and OHKO her back with +2 Magical Leafs, or +2 Mega Drains for healing. Again, keep Oricorio in mind.
  • If you used the mono Steel password, {{ sp("ARON") }} with Rock Throw and Metal Claw is super effective.

Her most dangerous Pokémon is the Oricorio which can set up Tailwind and completely shit all over you - especially if you picked a Grass starter. Make sure to eliminate it as soon as possible.

{{ trainer_battle_content("Julia", "JULIA", 20) }}

Julia will give you the Volt Badge, TM57 Charge Beam, the ability to use Cut outside of battle, and an increased level cap to Level 25. Before exiting the gym, set the weather to Stormy and go to the left with the radio dishes to find a wild {{ sp("BLITZLE") }}. We're almost done here in Peridot, but there's one missable event before we depart for South Obsidia.

Go back down to the riverside and talk to the hiker. He'll ask about places available to rent. Luckily... we do know one! It's the house where we previously found a Whismur. (If you didn't get the Whismur before, its now unavailable until Route 1 about twenty hours away. Oops!) Talk to the rent-seeker within; he's looking to let out the house to somebody. Go back to the hiker and tell him the good news. Finally, go back to the house and talk to the hiker again to get an {{ sp("ONIX") }}.

Now, you can make your way back into the Opal Ward and continue onwards to Obsidia ward.
