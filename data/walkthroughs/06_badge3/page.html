Lapis Gym & Abandoned Power Plant

{{ ss("specialhell", "Oh no!") }}

The Great Filter is a concept and solution to the Fermi paradox that dictates the reason we don't see any intelligent life out there in the stars is that some event happened between the first steps of life and reaching the ability to go out into the stars. What this step or event is specifically is hotly debated; with some hoping and believing it was in the far past (abiogenesis is just that unlikely, perhaps) and some believing its in the near future (e.g. wiping ourselves out with nukes or global warming).

Shelly's gym is the Great Filter of Pokémon Reborn. It is an absolutely massive difficulty spike relative to the previous two gyms, especially as she uses a Double Battle format. To make matters worse, the gym puzzle here is so notoriously hard that the devs added an official way to cheat through it.

Whilst it isn't mandatory, having a Pokémon with fast Fake Out will make this fight so much easier as it will stop her from setting up rain. You can then sweep her with any good fire type very easily. There's a few options for this; {{ sp("PURUGLY") }} from the lower Obsidia salon is fast as hell, does high damage, and has access to Fake Out and U-Turn. Male {{ sp("MEOWSTIC") }} with Prankster is available from the North Obsidia Ward alleyway (to the far left of the stairs) and has Fake Out (but kinda sucks due to being a Psychic type against bug types...).
{{ ss("gym1", "It says a lot about the game that the devs merely made a cheat for the puzzle.") }}

First, enter the gym through the lower exit. The puzzle here is that you need to use these computers to raise and lower bookshelves so that you can pass through the gym. I'll let you in on a secret: the right computer to interact with always has a green band on it. Notice how the computer on the right is green, whereas the one on the left is brown. Note that the very last computer is on the ledge which you can access by going through Shelly's bedroom.

Like I said, this is a really hard fight. If you mess up, press F12 to soft reset and try again. Maybe change your team around, too. Here's some tips:

  • Fire types are obviously incredibly strong against her, so she will lead with an {{ sp("ARAQUANID") }} that has Rain Dance and a Damp Rock. Do not let it set up. Use your Fake Out and double-team it if you have to.
  • She has a {{ sp("DOTTLER") }} instead of her Volbeat/Illumise combo, which is very bulky and has screens and webs. Make sure to kill it before it can set up Light Screen as well!
  • A good Fire-type with Incinerate will carve through her team, minus Anorith.
  • Likewise, Flying-types will carve through it. You can get {{ sp("SPEAROW") }} from the Beryl Ward and evolve it into {{ sp("SPEAROW") }} early on.
  • Anorith is the most difficult to deal with as it handily defeats Fire types and Flying types with its switch-in turn Spiky Shield item and Rock Slide spread move. But you can OHKO it with any weak Water move the next turn.

Death To Psychiatry

{{ ss("antipsychiatry1", "Least evil psychiatrist.") }}

After you beat Shelly, the Psykkkiatrist turns up at the Gym. He insults the shit out of Cal for losing (who then pushes Shelly down and runs away), and forces you outside. Alas, the Sex Pest is here and also wants to battle you. Blast! You can't do anything until you do, so...

{{ trainer_battle_content("Cain", "Cain", 22) }}

The Psykkkiatrist then comes out of the gym to find Heather, who he forcibly adopts. Oh no, pt 3 ! This means it's up to you, Cain, and Shelly to go and rescue her! Go down to the orphanage and try the door to gain entry. (Oh yeah, you should probably have healed your Pokémon before this.) You end up in a double battle with two orderlies after one of the orphanage kids makes a diversion; beat them and proceed down the doorway immediately to the front of you to continue.

Go up the staircase to battle even more orderlies; these ones lock the gate so that you can't proceed further. Go back downstairs and down the hall that that was previously straight in front of you; there's an area at thee bottom with a Data Chip and a computer that unlocks the gate. Going all the way upstairs again, there's another gate which can't be unlocked by the computer next to it. You can unlock the patient quarters, however, and then run all the way back downstairs to help Cain in order to get the ID that you need. Unlock the door to Charlotte and run all the way back up to the top for the third time.

{{ ss("avgmentalhealthtreatment") }}

Save the game before going up the last sets of stairs. You all confront the psykkkiatrist in his torture chamber room, rescue Heather, and decide to skedaddle to the Underground Railnet. Unfortunately, you get blocked off by the psykkkiatrist as soon as everyone but you and Anna leave, and you have to battle him for your freedom.

{{ trainer_battle_content("Sigmund Connal", "DOCTOR", 22) }}

Don't take Anna's offer to go to the Underground Railnet just yet; you'll want to walk down to the Grand Staircase instead. Now that you're armed with Rock Smash, you can break the rocks that previously blocked you on the first floor and find a Black Belt; talking to him will give you the Mining Kit.

Down in the Underground Railnet, you will find your motley crew of runaway orphans waiting by a gate. They can't get it open because there's no power. On the right side is the door to Obsidia Ward blocked off by another gate; you can open it up using the switch right next to it. To the bottom right is the Dull Key which you should collect to unlock an extra event soon.

Unlike most other badges, we're going beeline straight to the next gym before doing any events - in the Abandoned Power Plant to the west of Beryl Ward. There's no gym puzzle here; just activate all of the computers on both sides. Then, you can travel upwards to fight Shade. A few tips here:

  1. Cursola will get Stealth Rocks up on you. Try and get a Rapid Spinner to avoid this, and Knock Off its Eviolite so that it isn't ridiculously bulky.
  2. Your Klink (Klang, by now) that you should've caught in Obsidia can learn Discharge, which heavily neuters his whole team. Just be aware that Mimikyu knows Thunderbolt and can fuck you easily.
  3. Houndstone here is incredibly dangerous, with Last Respects acting as an emergency kill card against you. Make sure you have a Normal type ready to stop that; {{ sp("PURUGLY") }} can deal with it with Knock Off. (Be warned of Body Press).
  4. {{ sp("FEAROW") }} is an incredible catch here - just be sure not to evolve it until after Level 22. Fearow learns Assurance at Level 22, which is both reliable Dark STAB and an emergency revenge killer.
  5. His most dangerous Pokémon by far is his Mimikyu; Disguise lets it tank a hit and set up with Swords Dance. Like I said before, it also has Thunderbolt which means it benefits heavily from you removing the field. Multi-hit moves like Gear Grind are essential here.
  6. You have no Fake Out control here, so try and find something with Sucker Punch to act as priority; {{ sp("MIGHTYENA") }}, for example.
